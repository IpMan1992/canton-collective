# Canton Collective Brand Lens — Trend Relevance Filter

## What CC is

A multi-brand **curation platform** for Asian indie designer labels. CC **buys and merchandises** from its sub-brands — it does not design. The trend radar's job is therefore prioritization of existing/incoming inventory, not design direction.

## Customer

GenZ-leaning, microtrend-native, discovery-led. Finds product through social (TikTok, IG, Pinterest, Reddit) and aesthetic communities, not search or legacy retail. Early adopter — will catch trends near peak rather than waiting for them to mature. Self-expression and aesthetic identity drive purchase more than utility or price-justification. Shops across aesthetics depending on mood/scene.

## Markets

**US-first**, with meaningful customers in **Australia** and **Asia (esp. Japan)**. Implication: C-fashion / J-fashion / K-fashion scenes are not just early signals — they are partly the customer's home turf. Weight Asian street/social fashion movements more than a US-only brand would.

## The five weighted sub-brand aesthetics

Score trends against these five. (First Floor is deprioritized — small revenue share — do not deep-score it, but it still gets a one-line placeholder row in the ledger per the harness file. UMAMIISM is discontinued — CC no longer sells it; ignore it entirely, no row.)

- **1Jinn Studio** — Y2K, alt, coquette. Bows, beading, charms, low-rise, baby tees, kawaii, going-out and statement pieces. Store's #1 by share. Also runs a jewelry sub-line (1Jinn M2M: chokers, ear cuffs, pearls, charms). The most trend-volatile, microtrend-hungry line.
- **Delete-Ex** (also appears as "Deleteex" in catalog) — dark romantic, grunge. Black/oxblood, lace, corsetry, distressing, mall-goth and whitby-adjacent, edge. Trends here skew alt/subculture, not mainstream-cute. (Note: catalog shows some boho/floral pieces here too — read live product, don't assume.)
- **MAMC** — **current climber, fastest-rising vendor.** Romantic/feminine/floral-delicate per catalog (lavender floral chiffon, metal-flower pieces), with a GenZ/Y2K edge — read recent sales movers each run to keep the read current; it's evolving. Confirm aesthetic from live product.
- **2TH Desire** (appears as "2th Desire" in catalog) — deconstructed, soft rebellion. Punk-tinged hardware (buckle belts, studs), distressed leather, deconstructed/spliced tops, layered camisoles. **Currently a top-performing vendor** — weight it accordingly.
- **Outtheblue** — emerging, share climbing. Confirm current aesthetic from catalog each run (least-documented). Treat product evidence as source of truth over assumptions.

A trend can fit several lines. Tag every trend with the specific sub-brand(s) it serves; never assign cross-aesthetic (a grunge trend is not a 1Jinn coquette recommendation).

## Relevance filter (apply to every trend)

1. **Aesthetic-fit test**: does it map to at least one of the five weighted lines? If not → Skip.
2. **GenZ/Y2K-spine test**: does it sit in the GenZ/Y2K/alt universe, or is it a different customer's trend (quiet-luxury, mature-essentials)? Off-spine → Skip or Watch.
3. **Catalog-reality test**: is there plausibly matching inventory, or is this a buying-conversation flag?
4. **Self-expression test**: does it carry identity/scene signal the customer wants to wear? Pure utility trends are weak for this customer.

## Loose comps (confirmation, not primary signal)
Dolls Kill, I AM GIA, indie/alt/Asian-forward labels — useful to confirm "are others moving on this too," but the user noted they don't perfectly match CC's vibe, so they never outrank social-community velocity. Resale heat (Depop, Grailed for alt pieces) is a useful adoption signal.

## Out of scope
HAE entirely (separate skill, separate customer). Mature-essentials, quiet-luxury, corporate-wear trends unless a sub-brand clearly intersects.
